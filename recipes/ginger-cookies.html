Ginger Cookies

Soft ginger cookies to spice up the holiday season!

This recipe is for gingerbread cookies full of molasses, ginger, and other holiday spices. They'll stay soft for days in air-tight container, so the festivities can last well into the new year!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 1/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 large egg, room-temp
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 1 tablespoon water

Steps

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Set 2 tablespoons sugar in a small bowl; set aside.
  2. Sift together flour, ginger, baking soda, cinnamon, cloves, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Beat butter and remaining 1 cup sugar in a large bowl until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in egg, then stir in molasses and water.
  5. Gradually stir the sifted ingredients into the molasses mixture until well combined.
  6. Use floured hands to shape dough into 24 walnut-sized balls.
  7. Roll each ball in the reserved sugar until coated.
  8. Place cookies 2 inches apart on non-greased cookie sheets and flatten slightly with the bottom of a glass.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 8 to 10 minutes, switching racks halfway through.
  10. Remove from the oven and allow cookies to cool on the baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  11. Enjoy!
